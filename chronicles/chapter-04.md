# ⚔️ Chapter 04: The Warning

## The Peace

The Guard Posts changed everything.

Within days of their construction, the streets breathed easier. The patrols were visible — a guard at every corner, a watch on every lane. Thefts stopped. Fights dissolved before they could start. Suspicion gave way to trust as the guards got to know the people they protected.

The Court House stood ready to judge fairly. The Gatehouse logged every visitor at dawn and dusk. The Town Crier announced verdicts and warnings across the square. The Watchers had done their work well.

The kingdom, at last, felt safe.

Merchants brought their wares without fear. Farmers left their tools by the fields overnight and found them untouched at sunrise. Children played past dusk, something no one would have dared a month ago.

The Warrior sat on the steps of City Hall, watching the evening settle over the valley. The purple glow of the lanterns — velfire-dusted, the trader's gift — cast a warm light across the cobblestones. Music drifted from the Tavern. Laughter followed.

For one brief, beautiful moment, it felt like the kingdom had finally made it.

## The Light

It started as a flicker on the eastern road.

A barmaid leaving the Tavern was the first to see it. She squinted, set down her tray, and pointed.

"Someone's coming."

The word spread like wildfire. People gathered at the edges of the square, craning their necks. The light grew — not one, but dozens. Torches. Lanterns. Rows upon rows of them, bobbing in the darkness like a river of fire advancing toward the city.

The Guard Post sent word to the Guard Station. The Town Crier climbed his platform. But before any order could be given, the riders were already at the gates.

They did not stop.

Horses thundered through the Gatehouse, past the Guard Post, into the main square. The people scrambled aside as the cavalry fanned out — fifty men in steel and leather, their horses snorting steam into the cold night air. Each rider carried a staff, a blade at the hip, and a torch that cast long, dancing shadows across the City Hall walls.

They formed a semicircle before the steps and halted. The only sound was the stamp of hooves and the crackle of flame.

The people of the OOP Kingdom stood frozen, watching.

## The Scroll

One man dismounted. He was tall, draped in the grey-and-crimson of the old kingdom's heraldry. He unrolled a scroll, cleared his throat, and read in a voice that carried across the entire square.

> *"People of this city. We are from the POP Kingdom, representing the great king. Since you have all been banished from our kingdom, we are not supposed to concern ourselves with you. But in recent days, traders bound for our markets have grown scarce. They have been coming here instead. You are trading with our merchants, buying what should be ours, selling what we have forbidden."*

He paused, letting the weight of it settle.

> *"We leave you this warning: Cease all trade with the outer world. Stop receiving caravans. Stop selling velfire. Do this, and we will let you wither in peace. Refuse, and you will face catastrophe."*

The herald rolled the scroll and stepped back.

The crowd erupted.

## The Voice

Murmurs became shouts. Fear tangled with anger. Some demanded answers. Some demanded the riders leave. A few backed away, pale-faced, already imagining the worst.

Then, cutting through the noise like a blade, a voice:

> *"And what will you and your kingdom do if we don't stop?"*

Silence fell instantly.

The cavalrymen scanned the crowd, searching. One of them dismounted, hand on his sword hilt, and took a step toward the people. Before he could cross three paces, a hand stopped him from behind.

A figure emerged from the torchlight — taller than the others, broad-shouldered, his face carved from stone. His armor was dark, scarred from a hundred battles. He did not raise his voice. He did not need to.

**General Phir.**

*"We will wage war against your tiny city,"* he said, the words dropping like hammer blows. *"And we will smash you all into the ground."*

He turned and began walking back to his horse.

No one in the crowd dared breathe.

Phir reached for the saddle. And then — the same voice again, steadier this time:

> *"We will defend this city."*

The general stopped. He turned slowly, searching for the speaker. The crowd parted.

A young man stood there. His clothes were simple — a tunic, boots worn from work. He had no armor, no weapon. But behind him, one by one, others stepped forward. A woman with a bow. A boy barely old enough to hold a sword. A blacksmith's apprentice gripping a hammer. A dozen, then two dozen, then more.

*"We will stand and fight,"* the young man said. *"We will defend this city with our lives."*

General Phir studied them for a long moment. Then a thin smile crossed his face — not admiration. Amusement.

He mounted his horse without a word. He raised his hand, and the cavalry turned as one. They rode out of the square, through the gates, and disappeared into the darkness from which they had come.

The city stood in silence, listening to the hoofbeats fade.

## The Call

Dawn came slowly.

The Warrior stood before the gathered people. Her face was tired, but her eyes burned with the same fire that had led the thirty-seven across the wilderness.

"The Barracks has stood empty long enough," she said. "We trained a few guards — enough to patrol streets. But guards are not soldiers. We need an army."

She looked at the young people who had stepped forward — and the hundreds more who now stood behind them.

"We need archers — swift, accurate, deadly from a distance. We need a place to train them."

"We need explosives — gunners who can turn the tide with fire and powder. An arena to train them."

"And we need siege engines — catapults that can hurl stone and flame at any army that dares march on our home. A workshop to build them."

She raised her voice.

"POP thinks we are a tiny city that will crumble. Let us show them what this tiny city is made of."

---

*And so the fourth week of the OOP Kingdom began — not in prosperity, but in the shadow of war. The kingdom had built for peace. Now it must build for survival. The army is being raised. The enemy has drawn the line. And somewhere in the darkness, General Phir is waiting.*

**⚔️ Week Four is open. Builders, arm yourselves.*
